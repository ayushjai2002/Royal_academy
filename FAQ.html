ADMISSIONS:
  • 2. Which Academic Board is Royal Academy, Lalitpur affiliated to?
    • The school is to be affiliated with CBSE (Central Board of Secondary Education).
  • 3. How do Royal Academy differ from other schools?
    • ROYAL ACADEMY is a separate school that follows the International Baccalaureate program.
  • 4. What are the age criteria for admissions?
    • 2+years as of 1st April of the year, seeking admission in Pre-Nursery.
  • 5. Is there any possibility of relaxation in the age criteria?
    • We abide by Government norms for the age criteria, however, a 45-days age relaxation can be considered.
  • 6. What are the admission criteria?
    • Pre-Nursery, Nursery, KG-Interaction
      Class I- IX- Merit and Interaction
      Class XI- Written Test and Interaction
  • 7. What are the documents required at the time of admission?
    • @ Birth Certificate
      @ Residence Proof
      @ Progress report of last two years (Class II upwards)
      @ Two latest passport-sized colored photographs
      @ Transfer Certificate (from Class II onwards)
  • 8. How do I get information about the admission procedure?
    • Call on the given phone numbers during school hours.
  • 9. Does the school have a reserved quota for transferable cases?
    • Admissions are subject to the availability of seats. However, we prioritize and try our best to accommodate the students of transfer cases.
  • 10. Does the school take admissions in X and XII?
    • Coming Soon ... Admissions in grades X and XII will be granted based on the availability of seats and as per the policy and approval of CBSE for Direct Admission. There is a fee applicable as per the CBSE guidelines to process the admission.
ACADEMIC:
  • 11. What is the student-teacher ratio?
    • The student-teacher ratio is 7:1.
  • 12. What is the teaching methodology/approach followed by the school?
    • The school promotes a blend of Experiential, interrogative, and Project-based learning with a focus on all aspects of development like cognitive, linguistic, numeracy, general awareness, life skills, social skills, and ethical values.
  • 13. What syllabus does the school follow?
    • We follow a broad and balanced curriculum based on the National curriculum framework to provide holistic education. The school follows the guidelines as outlined in NEP 2020.
  • 14. How are students assessed?
    • The school follows an assessment system of Continuous and Comprehensive Evaluation for Primary and Middle classes with grades/marks. We follow CBSE guidelines for the classes.
  • 15. What are the second language options in class IX?
    • In class IX, other than the main subjects that are English, Mathematics, Science, and Social Science, the child has a choice of the second language. He/She can choose from Hindi & Sanskrit. The child must have studied these languages up to grade VIII and have cleared the exam with 33% marks.
